About me

I am a senior at Yale, completing a B.S. degree in Physics.

Currently, I am working for the National Oceanic and Atmosphereic Administration (NOAA) Earth System Research Laboratory (ESRL) in Boulder, Colorado. Our goal at ESRL is to gain a comprehensive understanding of the earth system. When I started at NOAA in 2015 I joined a group researching cloud processes with the aide of a numerical model. The numerical model creates realistic computer-generated clouds which we can then study in far greater detail than would be possible in the real world. Specifically we are pursuing a deeper understanding of low clouds. Because clouds cover nearly two-thirds of the world's surface at any given time it is essential that we understand their processes if we want to understand the Earth system as a whole.

In my first year at NOAA I worked on implementing high-order Weighted Essentially Non-Oscillatory (WENO) numerical schemes into the model my group uses. When the equations of physics are implemented in a numerical model they must be discretized. There are many ways to discretize a given equation but none faithfully represent the original continuous equation perfectly. Two properties we would like the discretized versions of equations to maintain are conservation of mass and conservation of energy.

In my second year I worked on the properties of trade cumulus clouds. Specifically I worked on understanding the power law relationship exibited in the size distribution of cumulus clouds and how this robust feature could be applied to a simple model of cumulus clouds.

This year my work focuses on stratocumulus clouds and a deeper understanding of the power law behavior exhibited by cumulus clouds

Outside of work I enjoy being outdoors. I love hiking and being by the Colorado Rocky Mountains.
