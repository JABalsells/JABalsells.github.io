About me

I am a senior at Yale, completing a B.S. degree in Physics.

Currently, I am working for the National Oceanic and Atmosphereic Administration (NOAA) in Boulder, Colorado, where I have worked since 2015. I work with a group that studies cloud processes using a numerical model called the System for Atmospheric Modeling ( SAM ). Specifically we are interested in the impact low clouds have on the global energy budget. Our approach is to focus on a top-down understanding of cloud properties.



My Research

Analysis of albedo versus cloud fraction relationships in liquid water clouds using heuristic models and large eddy simulation

A paper written in collaboration with Graham Feingold, Franziska Glassmeier, Takanobu Yamaguchi, Jan Kazil, and Allison McComiskey, published in the Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres, on deriving a simple analytic model to represent the albedo - cloud fraction relationship.
