About me

I am a senior at Yale, completing a B.S. degree in Physics.

Currently, I am working for the National Oceanic and Atmosphereic Administration (NOAA) Earth System Research Laboratory (ESRL) in Boulder, Colorado. ESRL was formed to pursue a broad and comprehensive understanding of the Earth system. When I started at NOAA in 2015 I joined a group which studies cloud processes using a numerical model to create realistic computer-generated clouds. Because we use a numerical model we have access to much more detailed measurements than would be possible with traditional measurement devices such as weather balloons and radar. Specifically we are interested in the impact low clouds have on the global energy budget. Our approach is to focus on a top-down understanding of cloud properties which is somewhat independent of many complicated details.

Outside of work I enjoy being outdoors.
